\section*{A}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item 
    Combinatorics, the study of arrangements of objects, is an important part of discrete mathematics.
    \textbf{True} \hspace{1cm} \textbf{False}
    \item 
    Match the tasks $(a,b,c, \ldots)$ with the procedures $(i,ii,iii,\ldots)$:
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Choosing one of 5 colors to paint walls or one of 4 wallpapers for the walls
        \item Eating one of 5 fish-dishes or one of 3 vegetarian dishes at a restaurant
        \item Picking one of 5 wedding venues or eloping to Las Vegas
        \item Buying a shack in the woods, sleeping in your office, or buying one of 4 affordable homes in Denver
    \end{enumerate}
    \begin{enumerate}[label=\roman*.]
        \item Choosing one of 9 ways to finish the walls
        \item Selecting one of 6 housing options
        \item Choosing one of 8 dinner options
        \item Picking one of 6 wedding options
    \end{enumerate}
    \item 
    Match the tasks $(a,b,c, \ldots)$ with the procedures $(i,ii,iii,\ldots)$:
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Pick one of 4 tops and one of 7 dresses
        \item Pick one of 4 Majors and one of 6 Minors
        \item Paint the walls one of 10 colors and the ceiling one of 4 colors
        \item Assign Cooking to one of 5 people and Washing to one of the remaining 4 people
    \end{enumerate}
    \begin{enumerate}[label=\roman*.]
        \item Choose one of 28 ways to get dressed
        \item Assign Kitchen duties in one of 20 ways
        \item Paint your house in one of 40 ways
        \item Choose one of 24 ways to get a degree
    \end{enumerate}
    \item 
    Let \( A = \{1, 2, \cdots, 5\} \) and \( B = \{1, 2, \cdots, 8\} \).
    How many different functions \( f : A \to B \) exist?
    \item 
    Let \( A = \{1, 2, \cdots, 5\} \) and \( B = \{1, 2, \cdots, 8\} \).
    How many different \textbf{injective} functions \( f : A \to B \) exist?
    \item 
    If \( |A| = 4 \), \( |B| = 2 \), and \( |C| = 3 \),
    then what is \( |A \times B \times C| \)?
    \item
    Consider the following disjoint sets:
    \[
    |A| = 4, \quad |B| = 2, \quad |C| = 3
    \]
    Then what is \( |A \cup B \cup C| \)?
\end{enumerate}
