\section*{C}
\begin{enumerate}
\item
A hotel has 15 floors and 42 rooms on each floor.  
How many total rooms are there in the hotel?
\textit{Hint: Apply the Product Rule — one choice for floor and another for room per floor.}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\roman*.]
    \item \( 15 + 42 \)
    \item \( 15 \times 42 \)
    \item \( 15^{42} \)
    \item \( 42^{15} \)
\end{enumerate}
\item
How many bit strings are there of length 8?
\textit{Hint: Each position in the string can be either a 0 or a 1 — apply the Product Rule.}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\roman*.]
    \item \( 8! \)
    \item \( 2^8 \)
    \item \( 8^2 \)
    \item \( 16 \)
\end{enumerate}
\item
How many different 3-letter initials can people have if none of the letters are repeated?
\textit{Hint: Use the Product Rule with decreasing options per letter.}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\roman*.]
    \item \( 26^3 \)
    \item \( 26 \times 25 \times 24 \)
    \item \( 3 \times 26^2 \)
    \item \( 24^3 \)
\end{enumerate}
\item
How many strings of 4 lowercase letters are there if at least one of the letters must be an \texttt{x}?
\textit{Hint: Use the complement principle. First find the total number of 4-letter strings, then subtract those with no \texttt{x}.}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\roman*.]
    \item \( 26^4 \)
    \item \( 25^4 \)
    \item \( 26^4 - 25^4 \)
    \item \( 4 \times 26^3 \)
\end{enumerate}
\item
How many subsets of a set with 20 elements have more than one element?
\textit{Hint: Use the total number of subsets and exclude those with 0 or 1 element.}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\roman*.]
    \item \( 2^{20} \)
    \item \( 2^{20} - 1 \)
    \item \( 2^{20} - 20 - 1 \)
    \item \( 20^2 \)
\end{enumerate}
\item
How many numbers must be selected from the set \( \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6\} \) to guarantee that at least one pair of these numbers adds to 7?
\textit{Hint: Apply the Pigeonhole Principle — think of complementary pairs that sum to 7.}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\roman*.]
    \item \( 2 \)
    \item \( 3 \)
    \item \( 4 \)
    \item \( 5 \)
\end{enumerate}
\item
How many strings of length 6 can be formed from the uppercase letters of the English alphabet?
\textit{Hint: Each position can be any of the 26 letters. Use the Product Rule.}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\roman*.]
    \item \( 6! \)
    \item \( 26^6 \)
    \item \( 26 \times 25 \times 24 \times 23 \times 22 \times 21 \)
    \item \( 26^3 \)
\end{enumerate}
\item

\end{enumerate}
